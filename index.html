About Me

My name is Ransmond Berchie, and I am a Ph.D. Biostatistics student at the Population Health Science Department of University of Utah. I am honored to be advised by Dr. Daniel Scharftein and Dr. Tom Greenefor my dissertation. As part of my doctoral studies, I also serve as the lead/secondary analyst in Dr. Adam Bress's lab. I am broadly interested in causal inference, survival analysis, longitudinal data analysis, and pharmacoepidemiology.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology-Kumasi, Ghana (KNUST). After serving as a Teaching Assistant for one year at KNUST, I moved to Northern Arizona University (NAU) for my Master's degree in Statistics.

 

Notable Hornors and Awards

  • Graduate Statistician Accreditation - American Statistical Association (2023)
  • Lester R. Curtin Award - American Statistical Association (2022)
  • African American Doctoral Scholars Initiative Award - University of Utah (2020)
  • Carlson-Diekelman Rushall-Schroeder Memorial Endowed Scholarship - Northern Arizona University (2019)
  • Outstanding Graduate GPA - Northern Arizona University (2019)
